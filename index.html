Take care of plants

Now the most significant extinction of plant and animal species in the last 65 million years is taking place, degradation and death of many valuable resource communities are observed: first of all, tropical forests, in which up to 200 species of woody plants alone can be found on an area of 1 hectare, not counting thousands of animal species.

The rate of extinction of species exceeds the natural course of evolution by an average of 5000 times. According to various estimates, up to 10 million species exist on Earth. With this rate of species extinction, it is highly likely that half of the species of terrestrial organisms could become extinct in the next 50 years. Every year, the list of rare plants listed in the Red Book is replenished.

The Red Book, in addition to the legal component, shows the current state of plants and animals in a particular region Plants of the Red Book - plants that we may no longer see, these are species that require special treatment from us. Unfortunately, the protection measures being developed do not always bring the expected result. Saving rare plants without changing our self-consciousness is not possible.

Ivy Pastukhov

  • Kingdom: Plants
  • Department: Angiosperms
  • Class: Dicotyledons
  • Order: Parasols
  • Family: Araliaceae
  • Genus: Ivy
  • Species: Ivy Pastukhov

Category and status: 2a - a species that is declining in numbers. Representative of an oligotypic genus of an ancient family. In Russia, it is located on the northern border of the range.

A brief description of. Evergreen woody vine. Attaches to tree trunks with adventitious roots or spreads along the ground (1). Reproduction in natural conditions is predominantly vegetative, rarely seed.

Galosciastrum Teelinga

  • Kingdom: Plants
  • Department: Angiosperms
  • Class: Dicotyledons
  • Order: Parasols
  • Family: Umbelliferae
  • Genus: Halosciastrum
  • Species: Galosciastrum Teelinga

Category and status: 2 a - a species that is declining in numbers as a result of habitat destruction. Representative of a monotypic genus, located in Russia at the northern limit of the range. A brief description of. Juvenile polycarpic herbaceous plant 50-60 cm tall. Blossoms in August. Reproduction is exclusively seed.

Fruiting is poor. Spreading. It is found only in the Khasansky district of Primorsky Krai in the Kedrovaya Pad nature reserve, as well as near the village. Narva (from where the species was described), Barabash on the river. Mongugay (Barabashevka), the sources of the river. Mongugay, top. R. Adimi, Sidimi (Blue Cliff), at the foot of the Moon. Outside of Russia, it grows in China and on the Korean Peninsula (1-3).

Yakut bell

Category and status: 3a - a rare species. Endemic to Russia (southeast of Yakutia). A brief description of. Herbaceous perennial up to 40-50 cm tall with a radish-like root, alternate leaves and few-flowered racemes (1, 2). Flowering in July-August, fruiting in August-September. Seed reproduction. Spreading. Found only in Russia.

Known from the southeast of the Republic of Sakha (Yakutia) from the Uchursky district near the border with the Khabarovsk Territory. Known locations are located along the banks of the river. Uchur (right tributary of the Aldan) between ss. Chagda and Chulbyu, in the areas of the mouths of the river. Gonam, Berdyakit, Megyuskian,

Sivagli, as well as along the right bank of the Aldan up to 100 km below the village. Chagda at the mouth of the Uchur (2, 3). The species was described from the Uchursky district of Yakutia (along the Uchur near Muguskan, the Megyuskyan river) (1-5).

Ginseng

  • Kingdom: Plants
  • Department: Angiosperms
  • Class: Dicot
  • Order: Parasols
  • Family: Araliaceae
  • Genus: Ginseng
  • Species: Panax ginseng

Category and status: 1 - endangered species. Tertiary relic, the only representative of the genus in Russia. In Russia, it is located on the northern border of the range.

A brief description of. Perennial herbaceous polycarp, with a thickened main tap root, usually branched. Under natural conditions, it reproduces mainly by seeds that germinate in the 2-3rd year, in rare cases - vegetatively (1, 2). The seeds are spread by birds. Young plants develop very slowly. The first flowering occurs in the 8-10th year. An important feature of biology is the ability to sink into a state of secondary dormancy from one to several decades under the influence of unfavorable factors. The total lifespan of a plant under natural conditions can reach 100-140 years (3).
